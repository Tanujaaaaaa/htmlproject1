Machu Picchu

The Hidden City of the Incas

High in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu was built by the Incas in the 15th century. Its terraced slopes and stone architecture remain an architectural marvel, with its true purpose still debated.

  • Historical Background: Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Incan city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It was built by the Inca emperor Pachacuti and abandoned during the Spanish conquest in the 16th century.

  • Rediscovery: The city remained unknown to the outside world until it was rediscovered by explorer Hiram Bingham in 1911.

  • Significance: Machu Picchu is one of the most iconic archaeological sites and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its sophisticated dry-stone construction and stunning mountain views.
