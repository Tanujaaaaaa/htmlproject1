  • Historical Background: Pompeii was an ancient Roman city near modern-day Naples, Italy. It was buried under volcanic ash and pumice after the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD.

  • Rediscovery: Pompeii was rediscovered in 1599 and then extensively excavated in the 18th century, revealing remarkably well-preserved ruins, including buildings, frescoes, and even human remains.

  • Significance: Pompeii offers an extraordinary snapshot of Roman life frozen in time, making it one of the most important archaeological sites in the world.
