  • Historical Background: Troy was a city mentioned in Homer's Iliad, famous for its involvement in the Trojan War. It is thought to have been located in what is now Turkey.

  • Rediscovery: Archaeologist Heinrich Schliemann discovered the ruins of Troy in the 19th century, proving that the city was not just a myth but had a historical basis.

  • Significance: The excavation of Troy uncovered several layers of cities, with evidence of both the legendary war and earlier settlements.
