Cahokia

The Forgotten Native American City

Cahokia, located near present-day St. Louis, Missouri, was a massive Native American city with over 120 mounds. It flourished between 1050 and 1350 AD but was mysteriously abandoned. Its remains are now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Historical Background: Cahokia was a pre-Columbian Native American city located in present-day Illinois, USA. It was the center of the Mississippian culture, thriving from 600 to 1400 AD.

  • Rediscovery: Cahokia was abandoned by the time European settlers arrived, but its massive earthworks and mounds were rediscovered in the 19th century.

  • Significance: Cahokia was one of the largest urban centers in North America before European contact and had a population of up to 20,000 at its peak.
